H2: How Perplexity actually works
Perplexity is an answer engine not a 
search engine. It uses Retrieval-Augmented 
Generation (RAG) — it searches the web, 
retrieves relevant pages, and synthesises 
a direct answer with citations.

Key facts:
- 630 million searches per month (2026)
- Growing 45% month over month  
- Visits ~10 pages per query
- Cites only 3-4 sources in the answer
- Pro users skew high-income, high-intent
- Built on Bing's infrastructure + own index

This means: there is no "position 1-10" 
in Perplexity. You are either cited or you 
are not. The optimization goal is citation, 
not ranking.

H2: The 6 factors that determine 
Perplexity citations

Factor 1: PerplexityBot crawler access
If PerplexityBot is blocked in your 
robots.txt — nothing else matters. 
It cannot read your site.

Check: visit yourdomain.com/robots.txt
Fix: add User-agent: PerplexityBot Allow: /

Factor 2: Answer-first content structure
Perplexity extracts answers from the 
first 150 words of a page. 

Wrong: long introduction before the answer
Right: direct answer in sentence 1

"If a 200-word setup precedes the actual 
information, Perplexity extracts nothing 
useful and deprioritises the page."
— Harbor SEO, 2026

Factor 3: Schema markup
Organization schema, Article schema, 
FAQPage schema all signal structured, 
trustworthy content to Perplexity's 
extraction model.

Factor 4: Domain authority and citations
Perplexity weights domain authority 
heavily. Sites with 350,000+ referring 
domains average significantly higher 
citation counts. Getting cited on 
high-DR sites is the fastest path to 
Perplexity visibility.

Factor 5: Content freshness
Perplexity's index updates frequently. 
New, accurate content has an advantage 
over older pages on fast-moving topics.

Factor 6: E-E-A-T signals
Author credentials, original research, 
first-person experience — Perplexity's 
NLP identifies expertise signals and 
weights them in source selection.

H2: What Perplexity SEO is NOT

Common mistakes:
- Keyword-stuffed headers confuse 
  Perplexity's entity extraction model
- Affiliate-heavy content with multiple 
  CTAs gets deprioritised
- Generic content without specific 
  data points gets ignored
- Long introductions before the answer 
  mean Perplexity extracts nothing useful
